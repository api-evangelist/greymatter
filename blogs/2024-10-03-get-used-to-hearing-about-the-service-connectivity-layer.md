<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-large-font-size" id="ember8901">Redundancy, Resiliency, and Cybersecurity Oh My! </h2>



<p id="ember8901">As a technology leader, I frequently engage in conversations with peers, program managers, and executives about security, disaster recovery, and operational efficiency options for their enterprises.</p>



<p id="ember8902">Recently, a discussion about disaster recovery for cloud-based apps and services led to the topic of Infrastructure as Code (IaC). Version-controlled IaC, used to set up infrastructure resources, offers a fast and repeatable deployment methodology. People often call it “immutable and reproducible” because each redeployment generates a new resource stack, leaving the original unchanged.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-large-font-size" id="h-setting-the-standard">Setting the Standard</h2>



<p id="ember8903">IaC has become standard as enterprises adopt cloud and hybrid-cloud infrastructures, with teams evolving from traditional networking roles to platform teams. But did the conversation end too soon? Here’s my challenge to technology leaders: you must combine efficient recovery through IaC with resiliency and assured operational control, not just for infrastructure, but also for applications, APIs, and services. Without this, when restoring or scaling to additional instances, there&#8217;s a risk that access policies are overlooked and that audited communication between apps/services is lacking, which can create an exploitable endpoint—a vulnerability where attacks, improper access, or data compromise can occur.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-large-font-size" id="h-real-world-expertise">Real World Expertise</h2>



<p id="ember8904">In my experience, discussions around enterprise IT architecture often focus heavily on infrastructure, network security, and storage solutions. While critical, let’s not overlook service connectivity—especially when enterprises need full visibility into transactions across layers 4-7. API security is a shared responsibility across several roles in an organization and in previous roles, I’ve experienced firsthand how difficult it can be to determine if downtime was caused by network infrastructure or the applications and services themselves. Today, service connectivity is providing that clarity in those environments, and its importance is only growing as enterprises adopt zero-trust models.</p>
