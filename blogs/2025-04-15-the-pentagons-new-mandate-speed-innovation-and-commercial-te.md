<li>Commercial technology solutions are now prioritized over custom development</li>

<p>The White House&#8217;s Executive Order on April 9, 2025, titled <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/modernizing-defense-acquisitions-and-spurring-innovation-in-the-defense-industrial-base/?utm_source=chatgpt.com" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">&#8220;Modernizing Defense Acquisitions and Spurring Innovation in the Defense Industrial Base&#8221;</a>, isn&#8217;t just a policy update—it&#8217;s a rallying cry to break free from bureaucratic gridlock. The order demands speed, measurable outcomes, and commercial solutions over the slow, costly custom programs that have held back DoD and National Security. With global competitors like China moving fast, this EO empowers America to reclaim its technological edge through agility and innovation—exactly what we deliver at <a href="https://greymatter.io/">Greymatter.io</a>.</p>



<p>This directive shifts the DoD from pursuing compliance to delivering results. The message is clear: national security depends on adopting secure, scalable commercial tech now. Greymatter.io&#8217;s solutions—autonomous zero-trust enforcement, multicloud and hybrid connectivity, instant observability, and secure service&nbsp;&nbsp;communications—address this urgency directly, delivering mission ready infrastructure at the pace of today&#8217;s evolving threats.</p>

<p>The EO establishes a high bar: commercial solutions, per&nbsp;<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/modernizing-defense-acquisitions-and-spurring-innovation-in-the-defense-industrial-base/#section8" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Section 8(c)</a>, must be &#8220;funded by private investment.&#8221; We can no longer stitch together open source tools with government labor and call it innovation—only proven, privately developed tech makes the cut. Greymatter.io leads with our Zero Trust Networking (ZTN) platform and service connectivity in this space.</p>



<p>Our&nbsp;<a href="https://greymatter.io/platform/platform-overview/">ZTN platform</a>&nbsp;isn&#8217;t a theoretical project—it operates successfully across DoD and Intelligence Community networks today. Autonomous zero-trust enforcement secures every workload with mTLS encryption and granular policies, while the platform&#8217;s multicloud and hybrid connectivity seamlessly integrates AWS, Azure, GCP, Oracle Cloud, and disconnected Kubernetes clusters to maintain operational continuity. Through comprehensive real-time observability, the system provides instant visibility and audit trails across IL2 to Top Secret workloads, enabling operators to act at mission speed. We deliver the ready-now technology the EO demands to outpace rivals.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-large-font-size" id="h-from-zombie-programs-to-outcome-driven-delivery"><strong>From Zombie Programs to Outcome-Driven Delivery</strong></h3>



<p><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/modernizing-defense-acquisitions-and-spurring-innovation-in-the-defense-industrial-base/#section6" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Section 6</a>&nbsp;subjects every Major Defense Acquisition Program (MDAP) to a 90-day scrutiny. Programs running 15% behind schedule, over budget, or failing performance goals risk termination. This approach eliminates &#8220;zombie programs&#8221; that consume resources without delivering value.</p>



<p>Greymatter.io thrives in this results first environment. Our self governing service connectivity layer automates proxies, gateways, and control planes, reducing setup time and complexity by up to 80%. We&#8217;ve proven our effectiveness in <a href="https://greymatter.io/solutions/by-industry-public-sector/">Impact Level 2 to 6+ (IL2–IL6+) environments</a>, meeting DoD and NIST zero trust controls without manual configuration burdens. For defense missions, this translates to secure, scalable systems that deploy rapidly—critical for gaining advantage over adversaries.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading has-large-font-size" id="h-breaking-the-culture-of-bureaucracy"><strong>Breaking the Culture of Bureaucracy</strong></h3>



<p>The EO fundamentally rewires culture, not just processes.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/modernizing-defense-acquisitions-and-spurring-innovation-in-the-defense-industrial-base/#section5" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Section 5</a>&nbsp;calls for reshaping the DoD&#8217;s 183,000-strong acquisition workforce, rewarding bold choices and commercial tech adoption.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/modernizing-defense-acquisitions-and-spurring-innovation-in-the-defense-industrial-base/#section4" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Section 4</a>&nbsp;eliminates redundant regulations.As defense acquisition expert Christopher Booth points out in his recent analysis for the Defense Acquisition University, this empowers acquisition professionals to prioritize innovation over paperwork, matching the urgency of global competition.</p>



<p>This cultural shift gained immediate reinforcement with Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth&#8217;s April 10 memo&nbsp;<a href="https://media.defense.gov/2025/Apr/10/2003687449/-1/-1/1/SECRETARY-OF-DEFENSE-PETE-HEGSETH-UPDATE-ON-CONTINUING-ELIMINATION-OF-WASTEFUL-SPENDING-AT-THE-DOD.PDF" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">&#8220;Update on Continuing Elimination of Wasteful Spending at the DoD&#8221;</a>. The memo establishes concrete targets for wasteful spending reduction, with specific focus on legacy IT systems that duplicate commercial capabilities. Secretary Hegseth emphasizes that &#8220;every dollar spent maintaining outdated systems is a dollar not spent on warfighter readiness&#8221; and directs department wide adoption of commercial solutions that meet security requirements. This endorsement creates unprecedented momentum for platforms that deliver immediate capabilities without extensive customization.</p>



<p>Procurement professionals previously faced penalties for innovative thinking. Now, they receive authorization to select platforms like Greymatter. Our automation first approach maintains security integrity and configuration consistency without requiring custom rebuilds. With built-in compliance—<a href="https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/sp/800-207/final" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">NIST 800-207</a>, FIPS 140,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.defense.gov/News/Releases/Release/Article/3225919/department-of-defense-releases-zero-trust-strategy-and-roadmap/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">DoD ZTA 2.0</a>—we simplify adoption.</p>
